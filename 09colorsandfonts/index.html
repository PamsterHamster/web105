Many who have wanderlust as a dominant personality trait relate to this famous quote. It does not imply we lack a purpose in life--quite the contrary. The purpose, often misunderstood by those who don't experience extreme wanderlust, is to experience the world from multiple angles. This includes geographical, cultural, and often results in deep, introspective reflections.

True nomads with wanderlust often do not have a conventional job/house/family or other things valued by the traditional society they were raised in. However, these people often have superior multi-cultural understanding and sensitivity. If these types of people also became world leaders, World Peace would be a shoe-in.
