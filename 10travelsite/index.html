Many who have wanderlust as a dominant personality trait relate to this famous quote. It does not imply we lack a purpose in life--quite the contrary. The purpose, often misunderstood by those who don't have wanderlust, is to experience the world firsthand from multiple angles. This includes geographical & cultural aspects, often resulting in deep, introspective reflections and broad understanding of humanity.

True nomads with wanderlust often do not have a conventional job/house/family or other things valued by the traditional society they were raised in. However, these people often have an acute multi-cultural understanding and sensitivity. If these types of people also became world leaders, World Peace would be a shoe-in.
