Why Znojmo?

Znojmo is located in the southeastern region of Moravia, about 54km/34miles from Brno, near the Austrian border and is situated on the bank of the Thaya (Dyje) River. As of 2019, approximately 34,000 people live in the city. The area is known for wine vineyards and is host to many festivals (wine, art, music, etc.), usually located in the city center.

If you love wine, come during the designated weekend in September for the famous wine festival, where guests can purchase a punch pass and sample 20+ wines grown in the region. The local wine specialty is Burcak (harvested from young, fermented grapes). Lots of other festival activities will be going on simultaneously. The upcoming wine festival dates are September 11-13, 2020.


If historical sites are more your speed, then know Znojmo dates back to the Medevial times. The local castle dates back to the 12th century. Nearby is the stone Romanesque Rotunda of Saint Catherine, also dating back to the 12th century (more than 600 years before the discovery of the USA). The inside is covered with still in-tact frescoes depicting various biblical scenes.
