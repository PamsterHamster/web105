History

There are approx. 27Km/16.7 miles of underground tunnels around the city center of Znojmo, built during medevial times. Originally the tunnels were used for agricultural purposes ie. food storage, but became a very important place of refuge during wars.

There was only one way in and one way out of this labrynth--the river and city center are key. The rare times an enemy entered the maze, he hardly ever escaped alive.


WWII

Many Jews hid in the underground to avoid Nazi capture and persecution. Because the passages are very complex, the hidden Jews survived (if they hadn't succumbed to disease or other natural causes). As a result, the Nazis didn't stay in Znojmo long, because after searching extensively above ground, they concluded that there was nothing there for them.
