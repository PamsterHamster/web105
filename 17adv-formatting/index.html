The Power of Language

Music was my first language, first heard (and probably understood) in the womb, long before I ever learned to speak. Music is a global endeavor, a universal language. Music was the start of my entire sense of being, of the directions I’d take in life and what I’d ultimately become.

For my entire childhood I was immersed in the musical world of classical composers (aka the Dead People), mostly of European descent. Bach, Beethoven, Brahms, Debussy, Borodin and Ravel (to name just a few), have always sent me on a fascinating emotional and intellectual journey-much like being absorbed in the reading of a fantastic book. As a child,I had always wondered about the anthropology and circumstances surrounding the piano or orchestral work during its time of composition. Such questions absorbed me, such as: How were the circumstances similar or different to the current time? What did the composer’s town or village look like? What was the history behind the piece?

And yet, during my various quests of both imagination and some reading of the Encyclopedia Britannica, that was never enough. I knew someday I had to travel to those places, to see them in person, to get a feel of what those composers may have experienced during their time there. I was disciplined and voracious in my musical studies as a child and young adult, and it brought me amazing opportunities. I was a little Musicologist who never got the formal musicology degree, because I had other plans…plans which seemed to evolve naturally from my other interests and interdisciplinary values.

I have always had a sense of philanthropy, leadership, and marketing. Combined with my musical interests, this led me into Arts Management specializing in orchestras and later Linguistics (ESL/EFL), then International Business and Web Design learning. These areas at first might seem very disjointed from one another or unrelated. However,they are all tied together by language: the language of music; the language of learning/using a foreign language; the language of business transactions; and the language of computer programming and design.

Education is a lifelong endeavor, a fundamental process which should not stop simply because you have accomplished a degree or certificate. Rather, it is a journey of lifelong pursuit towards your purpose in the Universe, or fulfilling your ultimate purpose in life. For me, this has to do with interdisciplinary studies, or finding the common ground among seemingly otherwise disjointed things. It is my desire to work together with other experts from various fields to create effective interventions and solutions across cultures and societies, which has the potential and power to transform the educational landscape. By thinking with an interdisciplinary approach, one can find their way to develop meaningful solutions to complex problems.
